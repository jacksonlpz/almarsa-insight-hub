Trademark registration in the UAE is governed by Federal Decree-Law No. 36 of 2021 on Trademarks. The competent authority for registration is the Ministry of Economy & Tourism (MoET). A single registration covers all seven Emirates.
1. Key Facts and Requirements
Aspect	Detail
Governing Law	Federal Decree-Law No. 36 of 2021
Filing Authority	Ministry of Economy & Tourism (MoET)
Validity Term	10 years from the filing date, renewable for successive 10-year periods.
Foreign Applicants	Mandatory to file through a registered UAE trademark agent (Power of Attorney is required).
Classification	The UAE follows the Nice Classification (goods and services).
Multi-Class	Not permitted. A separate application must be filed for each class.
Non-Use Risk	Registration can be cancelled if the mark is not genuinely used within 5 consecutive years from the registration date.
Typical Timeline	Approximately 4 to 6 months if the process is smooth and unopposed.

2. Required Documents (for corporate/foreign entities)
When filing a trademark application, the following are typically required, usually provided to your local trademark agent:
1.	Trademark Logo/Specimen: A clear soft copy of the mark (word, design, or combined).
2.	Commercial License/Certificate of Incorporation: A copy of the applicant entity's business registration.
3.	Legalized Power of Attorney (POA): An original, notarized, and legalized POA is required. For foreign applicants, this document must typically be attested by the UAE Embassy/Consulate in the home country and locally by the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MoFA).
4.	Priority Document (if claiming priority from an earlier foreign application): A certified copy, along with its Arabic and English translation, must be submitted, usually within 90 days from the UAE filing date.
5.	List of Goods/Services: A precise list of the products and/or services to be covered, categorized according to the Nice Classification.

3. Step-by-Step Registration Process
The registration process involves several stages with associated fees and deadlines:
Step	Action	Fee (Approx. Official MoET Fee)	Description
1. Search & Strategy	Conduct a trademark clearance search to ensure the mark is distinctive and not similar to existing registered or pending marks in the relevant classes.	N/A (Agent fees apply)	Recommended to avoid rejection and opposition.
2. Filing	Submit the official application and required documents to the MoET.	AED 750 (Filing Fee)	Filing date is secured upon payment and submission.
3. Examination	The MoET Trademark Office examines the application against absolute and relative grounds for refusal.	N/A	MoET will notify the applicant of acceptance or refusal within 90 days.
4. Publication	If accepted, the mark is published in the Official Trademark Bulletin.	AED 750 (Publication Fee)	This initiates the opposition period.
5. Opposition Period	A mandatory 30-day period during which any third party can file an objection to the registration.	N/A (Legal fees apply for objection/response)	If an objection is filed, the process moves to a hearing or settlement phase.
6. Registration	If there is no opposition, or if the opposition is unsuccessful, the applicant pays the final fee and the certificate is issued.	AED 5,000 (Final Registration Fee)	The trademark is officially registered and protected.
Total Estimated Official Fees: AED 6,500 (excluding agent fees, legalization costs, or fines for late payment).
